*   **Friction Testing:** Applied a 10bps slippage penalty to ensure the edge wasn't a "Paper Tiger."

## 3. Key Takeaways & Lessons Learned

### The "Overfitting" Trap
*   **Lesson:** A rolling optimizer that picks the "perfect" Z-score for the last 3 years will almost always fail in the future (Overfitting Ratio > 2.0x).
*   **Fix:** Use **Parameter Parsimony**. Restrict the search space to a few "sane" values (e.g., 2.25, 2.5, 2.75) or use a fixed sector-wide constant.

### The "Sector Archetype" Discovery
*   **Lesson:** Mean-reversion is not a universal property; it is a **structural feature** of specific business models.
*   **Finding:** **REITs** (High $\theta$, Low $\sigma$) consistently outperformed Utilities and Staples because their "Dividend Gravity" creates a reliable mathematical anchor for price.

### The Danger of Correlation
*   **Lesson:** A portfolio of 8 tickers is not diversified if 3 of them are Residential REITs with 0.91 correlation.
*   **Fix:** Implement **Sector Capping**. Treat highly correlated assets as a single "Risk Unit" and limit exposure (e.g., Max 2 concurrent REIT positions).

## 5. Quantitative Playbook for Future Projects

1.  **Start with Physics:** Don't just look for patterns; look for the *mechanism* (e.g., Dividend Gravity, Inventory Cycles) that causes mean-reversion.
2.  **Audit the "Luck":** Always run a Permutation Test. If p > 0.05, the strategy is likely noise.
3.  **Penalize Complexity:** If a strategy needs 5+ parameters to work, it's overfit. Aim for 2-3 robust parameters.
4.  **Walk-Forward Everything:** Never validate on the whole dataset. Use purged, expanding windows to simulate live-trading conditions.
5.  **Respect Friction:** Always backtest with realistic slippage (10bps) and commissions. If the edge disappears, the strategy isn't ready for live capital.
